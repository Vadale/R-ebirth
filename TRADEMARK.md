# Trademark and Naming Policy

The R-ebirth **code** is free (MIT OR Apache-2.0). The R-ebirth **name** is not.

This follows the model used by Rust and Firefox: the source is open, but the
project name and identity are reserved so that users can trust that software
called "R-ebirth" or "rebirth" is the genuine, unmodified project.

## What you may do

- Use, modify, embed, and redistribute the code — including in proprietary
  products — under the terms of the MIT or Apache-2.0 license.
- State truthfully that your product is "built on", "powered by", or "derived
  from" R-ebirth.

## What requires renaming

- If you distribute a **modified** version of the package or its crates, you
  must **rename** it. The distributed artifact may not be called "R-ebirth" or
  "rebirth", may not use project logos, and may not imply endorsement by the
  project.

## No endorsement

R-ebirth is an independent project. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or
sponsored by the R Foundation, and it does not use the R Foundation's logo or
imply its endorsement.

Questions about permitted uses can be raised as an issue once the public
repository exists.
